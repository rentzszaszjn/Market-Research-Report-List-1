<p><p>The Food and Pharmaceutical Grade Propylene Glycol market consists of three key types: Hydration, Transesterification, and Biosynthesis. Hydration involves the reaction of propylene oxide with water, producing propylene glycol. Transesterification entails the reaction with fats or oils, yielding ester compounds and propylene glycol. Biosynthesis utilizes biological processes, often employing microorganisms to convert biomass into propylene glycol. Each method caters to different production needs, ensuring high-quality, safe products for food and pharmaceutical applications while addressing sustainability concerns.</p></p>
