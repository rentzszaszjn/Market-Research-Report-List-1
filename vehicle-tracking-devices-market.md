<p><p>Vehicle tracking devices utilize two main market types: cellular network and satellite. Cellular network tracking leverages mobile networks to transmit location data, offering real-time updates and wide coverage, particularly in urban areas. It is typically more cost-effective but relies on the presence of cellular infrastructure. In contrast, satellite tracking operates independently of terrestrial networks, providing global coverage and enhanced accuracy, especially in remote regions. Each method caters to different needs, influencing the choice of tracking solution based on operational requirements.</p></p>
